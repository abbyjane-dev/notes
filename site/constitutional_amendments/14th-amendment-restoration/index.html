Fourteenth Amendment Restoration¶

Section 1 — National Citizenship and Supremacy¶

The Fourteenth Amendment is reaffirmed as a guarantee of national citizenship and as a binding limitation on the States.
No State shall invoke sovereignty, reserved powers, dignity, or immunity as a basis to evade or diminish the rights secured by that Amendment.

Section 2 — Privileges or Immunities¶

The privileges or immunities of citizens of the United States include all rights secured by this Constitution and by laws enacted pursuant thereto.
No State shall abridge such privileges or immunities. The privileges or immunities secured by this Constitution, and by laws enacted pursuant thereto, shall apply to all persons and all territory subject to the jurisdiction or authority of the United States. No interpretation of this Constitution shall construe this clause narrowly or as without independent force.

Section 3 — Due Process¶

No State shall deprive any person of life, liberty, or property without due process of law.
This guarantee secures substantive as well as procedural protections and applies to all rights secured by this Constitution.

Section 4 — Equal Protection¶

The Equal Protection of the laws guarantees equal civil status and equal protection against arbitrary, discriminatory, or unequal state action.
No classification by a State shall be upheld unless consistent with this guarantee.

Section 5 — Incorporation¶

All rights enumerated in this Constitution are applicable to the States.
No distinction shall be drawn between incorporated and unincorporated rights.

Section 6 — Enforcement Power (Restored)¶

Congress shall have power to enforce the provisions of this Amendment by appropriate legislation.
Such power shall include the authority to define, prevent, remedy, and deter violations of this Amendment.
This authority shall not be limited by judicial doctrines requiring congruence, proportionality, narrow tailoring, or deference to state sovereignty.

Section 7 — Disqualification Clause Enforcement¶

The disqualification prescribed by Section 3 of the Fourteenth Amendment is self-executing.
No prior criminal conviction, impeachment, or congressional action shall be required for its enforcement.
Congress may by law provide procedures for the determination and enforcement of such disqualification, but the absence of such procedures shall not bar its application by any court or election authority with jurisdiction, notwithstanding the absence of implementing legislation.

Section 8 — Supersession¶

Any prior judicial interpretation inconsistent with this Amendment or with the original purpose and enforcement authority of the Fourteenth Amendment is hereby superseded.

 

Section 9 — Citizenship¶

All persons born in the United States and subject to its laws are citizens of the United States and of the State wherein they reside.

A person born in the United States shall be deemed subject to the jurisdiction of the United States at birth regardless of the citizenship, nationality, immigration status, or lawful presence of the person’s parents.

The phrase “subject to the jurisdiction thereof” excludes only persons born:

  1. to foreign diplomatic officers accredited to the United States;

  2. to persons exercising sovereign authority on behalf of a foreign power; or

  3. to members of an invading or occupying military force.

No other exception to birthright citizenship shall be recognized as a constitutional requirement.

Congress may by law provide for the acquisition of citizenship on terms more permissive than those set forth in this Section.
