# Pardon Power Accountability Amendment

## Section 1 — Covered Officers

The pardon power of the President shall not extend to any person who, at the time of the conduct for which pardon is sought:

<p text-indent= 50px>(a) held an office of the United States whose appointment required confirmation by the Senate;</p>
<p text-indent= 50px>(b) served in an acting, interim, or temporary capacity in any such office; or</p>
<p text-indent= 50px>(c) served in the Executive Office of the President or in any successor office designated by Congress, regardless of title or organizational form.</p>

## Section 2 — Specificity Requirement

Every pardon shall:

<p text-indent= 50px>(a) identify the individual by legal name;</p>
<p text-indent= 50px>(b) specify the conduct pardoned; and</p>
<p text-indent= 50px>(c) identify the federal statute or statues, including section numbers, to which the pardon applies.</p>

No pardon may use general, omnibus, or open-ended language such as “all offenses against the United States.”

## Section 3 — No Self-Amnesty and No Class Pardons

No President may pardon himself or herself.
No pardon may be issued to any class of persons defined by office, agency, function, or status.

## Section 4 — Effect of Violation

Any pardon issued in violation of this Amendment shall have no legal force.

## Section 5 — Preservation of Clemency

Nothing in this Amendment shall restrict the President’s power to grant pardons to persons not described in Section 1, except as limited by the requirements of Section 2.

## Section 6 — Effective Date

This Amendment shall take effect upon ratification.
