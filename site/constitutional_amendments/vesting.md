# Vesting Clause Amendment

## Section 1
The executive power of the United States is vested in the President and such officers, agencies, and instrumentalities as may be established by law. The vesting of executive power in the President shall not be construed to require that all persons exercising executive functions be subject to the President’s direction, supervision, or removal at will.

## Section 2
Congress may by law establish executive officers and agencies whose heads or members may be appointed for fixed terms and removed only for cause, or in such manner as Congress shall provide. The existence or exercise of executive authority by such officers or agencies shall not be deemed inconsistent with this Constitution.

## Section 3
No interpretation of this Constitution shall recognize an inherent or exclusive presidential authority to control, direct, or remove all executive officers solely by virtue of the Vesting Clause of Article II.

## Section 4
Any prior judicial interpretation inconsistent with this amendment is hereby superseded.
