# Judicial Access Amendment

## Section 1
The judicial power of the United States shall extend to all cases and controversies within the jurisdiction of the federal courts as established by this Constitution and by law. The requirement of a case or controversy shall be satisfied where adverse parties present a concrete dispute capable of judicial resolution.

## Section 2
No court of the United States shall decline to exercise jurisdiction otherwise properly conferred on the ground that a question presented is political, prudential, or inappropriate for judicial determination.

## Section 3
Congress may by law define and regulate the jurisdiction of the federal courts, including standards of standing, ripeness, and mootness. No judicially created doctrine shall limit such jurisdiction except as expressly provided in this Constitution.

## Section 4
Where jurisdiction is proper and facts sufficient to resolve a dispute are established, the courts of the United States shall decide the questions of law presented.

## Section 5
Any prior judicial interpretation inconsistent with this amendment is hereby superseded.
